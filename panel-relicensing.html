ElectrumSys relicenses source code ahead of version 2.6 release

As of February 24 2016, ElectrumSys source code is published under the MIT license, the same license used by Syscoin Core. This change concerns both the ElectrumSys client and the ElectrumSys server.

We decided to relicense ElectrumSys for two reasons: Firstly, we want to make ElectrumSys more attractive to developers who are looking to integrate lightweight Syscoin functionality into their systems, and a more permissive license is helpful in this regard. This is especially relevant for the ElectrumSys server, which was previously published under the restrictive GNU Affero Licence.

Secondly, we are about to release a new version of ElectrumSys for mobile devices and tablets. In order to make ElectrumSys available in various app stores (such as Microsoft's Windows Store or Apple's App Store) we cannot keep the restrictive GPL license, as publishing ElectrumSys on some of these stores may break the terms of the GPL.

Over the last few months, we have been collecting permission from the ElectrumSys contributors, in order to republish their contributions under the MIT license. More than 100 contributors agreed with the change. One contributor opposed the change, and a few others were unreachable; the relevant portions of the code were either removed (such as the GTK GUI) or rewritten.

I would like to thank all the developers and non-developers who contributed to ElectrumSys.

Thomas Voegtlin, founder of ElectrumSys.
